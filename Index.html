DABABY

DaBaby, whose real name is Jonathan Lyndale Kirk, was born on December 22, 1991, in Cleveland, Ohio, but grew up in Charlotte, North Carolina. He is an American rapper, songwriter, and entrepreneur who gained significant attention following the release of his debut studio album, "Baby on Baby," in 2019. This album marked his commercial breakthrough, especially with the Billboard Hot 100 top-ten single "Suge." His subsequent releases, including "Kirk" (also 2019) and "Blame It on Baby" (2020), further solidified his status as one of the leading figures in contemporary rap music. These albums produced numerous hits such as "Intro," "Bop," "Rockstar" featuring Roddy Ricch, and "Levitating" among others.
DaBaby has been nominated for several Grammy Awards throughout his career, including a nod for Record of the Year for "Rockstar." Despite facing public controversies, particularly around homophobia, he continues to be a prominent figure in the music industry. In addition to his music career, DaBaby founded Billion Dollar Baby Entertainment in 2017, a record label based in Charlotte, North Carolina, where he aims to cultivate new talent. The label has signed artists like Stunna 4 Vegas, whom DaBaby considers a protégé.
DaBaby's music often features collaborations with notable artists across various genres, reflecting his versatility and influence within the music scene. His work ethic, business acumen, and artistic contributions have made him a key player in modern hip-hop culture.
